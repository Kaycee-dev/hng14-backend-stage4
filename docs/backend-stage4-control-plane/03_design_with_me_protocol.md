# Design-With-Me Protocol

The goal is not only to submit a scaling design. The goal is to understand and defend every design choice under interview pressure.

## Before Each Design Slice

Record:

- what the slice adds to the final design document
- the system design concept behind it
- the exact files in scope
- the expected acceptance criteria
- the main failure mode to watch for
- the interview answer that justifies the approach

## During The Slice

- Explain important assumptions before relying on them.
- Keep changes within the active packet.
- Capture rejected ideas and why they were rejected.
- Do not move to adjacent work just because it is nearby.

## After The Slice

Record:

- evidence id and updated artifact paths
- what changed in plain English
- what the operator should be able to explain
- new interview questions and answers
- unresolved risks or follow-ups

## Teach-Back Gate

A slice can close only when the operator can explain:

- what problem the design element solves
- why the chosen approach fits the brief
- how to verify it in the final document
- where the design degrades or intentionally stays simple
